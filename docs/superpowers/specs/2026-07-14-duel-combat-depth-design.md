# Duel Combat Depth — Design Spec

**Status:** approved by owner 2026-07-14, ready to plan/implement.
**Supersedes nothing.** Extends the phase-1 duel (`docs/duels-prototype-spec.md`).

## The problem (owner: *"it's basically just target 1 enemy and that's it, who kills them fast. there is no skill"*)

Focus-fire isn't a strategy players discovered — **it is the only line the rules permit.** This is
Lanchester's square law with identical guns:

- A treat deals **100% of its damage until it hits 0**. So killing is the *only* way to reduce
  incoming damage. Concentrating damage compounds; spreading it does nothing.
- **No heal** → damage is permanent → racing is always correct.
- **No AoE** → spreading is never more efficient than concentrating.
- **No redirect/cover** → the focused treat is *undefendable* (Block only protects the blocker).
- The engine **actively subsidises** it: when your target dies, your attack auto-retargets onto the
  next lowest-HP enemy (`engine.cpp` retarget stage). **Overkill is refunded** — you cannot
  over-commit, so alpha-striking is risk-free.
- **All six treats are identical guns.** Damage is `move.power × clash`, and *does not scale with
  fighter quality or sweetness at all* — only HP does (`hp = 100 + (q-1)*30 + sw*10`). So a
  Legendary is just a Common that takes 2.6× longer to kill. There is no reason to prefer one
  target over another → target choice is a **lookup**, not a decision.

## Core principle

> ### Rarity buys VERBS, not NUMBERS.

Damage stays **flat across quality and sweetness, forever**. What a rare treat buys is a richer
**toolkit**. This does three jobs at once:

1. **Fairness / no pay-to-win.** A starter hits exactly as hard as a Legendary. Critical, because
   duels will eventually stake treats (winner-picks-treat / capture) — *"i dont think we should
   allow someone to play super expensive treats vs a noob, and lose those treats."*
2. **Threat heterogeneity.** Once rare treats carry exotic verbs, enemies stop being
   interchangeable — *"kill the one about to group-heal"* becomes a real thought. This is what makes
   target selection a trade-off instead of a lookup.
3. **It's already in the chain.** Fighter quality gates BOTH move count and move quality
   (`database/recipe.cpp`: a fighter only rolls moves whose `quality == fighter.quality`):

   | Fighter  | Moves owned | Move quality |
   |----------|-------------|--------------|
   | Common   | 3           | all q1       |
   | Uncommon | 5           | all q2       |
   | Rare     | 7           | all q3       |
   | Epic     | 9           | all q4       |

   **So "newbie treats have the simple attacks, better treats have better ones" needs ZERO on-chain
   change.** The duel engine simply ignores this today, treating q4 as "bigger number, longer
   cooldown".

## Feasibility headline: effects are FREE, memory is not

`DuelMoveStat` is a **compile-time table baked into the WASM blob** (sha-pinned, identical on both
sides of a future channel). State bytes only carry hp/max_hp/quality/sweetness/moves/cooldowns.

- **Heal, AoE, Guard, shield, siphon, counter → 0 new wire bytes, 0 new state bytes.**
- Only mechanics that must **remember something between rounds** (stun, poison DoT, buffs, taunt)
  cost state: ~4 × u8 per treat, `STATE_LEN` 104 → 128 at 3v3. (A signature is 65 bytes. This is
  nothing.)

### ⏳ The one deadline
The duel engine is **standalone today**: no GSP source references `duel/`, and `test/golden.json` is
a **gitignored build artifact** regenerated by `build.sh golden`. So changing the wire format costs
*one command*. **The moment game channels sign state bytes, the layout is frozen forever.**
→ **Bump `kVersion` once, NOW, and add the reserved status bytes even before we use them.**

## Wave 1 — stop subsidising the race (no new abilities)

These are **prerequisites, not optional**. Heals/AoE shipped *before* them would make focus-firing
**more** dominant, not less (only concentrated burst outruns a heal).

| Change | What | Degenerate strategy it kills |
|---|---|---|
| **Fizzle-on-death** | Delete the auto-retarget. An attack on a corpse **whiffs**; damage never carries over. | Risk-free alpha strike. Over-committing 3 treats into one target becomes a **bet you can lose**, and every enemy defensive action becomes a **bait**. |
| **Guard (Cover)** | A Blocking move may target an **ally**; the guardian intercepts single-target damage aimed at them (~75%). 0 wire bytes — the target byte already exists. | *"The focused ally is undefendable"* — the exact complaint. Now the attacker must guess whether the guard is up (cooldowns are public → **inference, not a coin flip**). |
| **Narrow the pentagon** | adv 1.5× → ~1.25×, dis 0.66× → ~0.80×. | *"The round is decided by a blind coin flip I can't influence."* ±50% swamps every other decision and reads as luck. |
| **Make the Blocking corner live** | Blocking moves carry an unused `power` field. Spend it: counter / shield / guard. | *"Nobody ever blocks, so the pentagon is really a 4-way and every read is noise."* Also resurrects 4 q4 Blocking moves that are currently **strictly worse than q1 blocks**. |
| **Sudden death** | From ~round 20, damage scales up each round. | The stall equilibrium that Guard/heals would otherwise create. **Also fixes the round-cap tiebreak, which is by total HP — the instant heals exist, turtling becomes a win button.** |

## Wave 2 — the verbs (0 wire cost)

Rare treats get these; **commons keep honest, reliable damage** (they are *not* weak — flat damage +
short cooldowns makes them the consistent DPS baseline).

- **AoE** — hits every living enemy at ~60% power (sublinear, so it's throughput-positive but
  spread). Clash is computed **per target** against each victim's own picked move. AoE **ignores
  Guard** (Guard intercepts single-target only), which makes it the designated counter to
  Guard/group-heal. With fizzle-on-death it becomes the one move that can cash **two kills**.
- **Heal** (ally-targeted) and **group heal**. **HARD RULE: a heal on a dead ally is an explicit
  no-op** — KO permanence rests entirely on the `hp == 0` guards; an unguarded heal is an accidental
  revive. Overheal is wasted.
- **Life siphon** (owner's ask) — deal damage, heal the attacker for a fraction. Elegant because you
  must *attack* to sustain: it can't turtle.
- **Shield / absorb** — flat HP absorbed before damage, persists until consumed. The exact **inverse
  of Block**: shield beats chip and loses to burst; Block (%) beats burst and loses to chip. Having
  both means the defender's choice depends on a read of the attacker, and the attacker's on a read
  of *that* read.
- **Counter** — attackers who damage me this round take a fraction back.

## Limited-use ultimates (replaces action points) — owner-decided

Owner floated Axie-style **action points**, then landed on the better idea: *"maybe dont need action
points, or some abilities can only use once if very powerful. or twice or something."* **Adopted.**

- A move gains a compile-time `max_uses` (0 = unlimited, the default). A signature q4 move gets
  **1–2 uses per duel, full stop.**
- Delivers the same tension as an energy economy ("can I afford this *now*?") and makes a signature
  move feel genuinely special — at a fraction of the cost. **Rejected action points** because
  cooldowns already ration per-treat, energy is a *second* overlapping economy, it needs a
  cross-treat budget rule (ugly in a blind simultaneous pick), and — decisively — **it does not fix
  focus-fire**: you would still focus one enemy, just on a budget. The research ranked an energy
  economy LAST on leverage-per-complexity.
- **State cost:** `u8 uses_left[loadout_size]` per treat (+4 bytes at L=4). Include it in the ONE
  wire bump. Also **reserve a byte for a future team energy pool** — costs nothing now, and keeps
  action points a pure engine change later instead of a breaking format change *after channels
  freeze the layout*.

## Crits, double-attacks and misses — EARNED, never rolled

Owner: *"maybe we need things like rare double attacks, or some treats have +% to double attacks? or
crits… and maybe sometimes you should miss"* — and, in the same breath, *"needs all be
deterministic etc."* Those two pull against each other. **Resolution: keep the drama, drop the dice.**

RNG would actively destroy the thing we are building. The whole point of this rework is to add
**skill**; dice launder skill away and merely relabel the grievance — from *"there's no skill"* to
*"I lost to a crit."* It is also genuinely expensive: real randomness in a game channel needs a
commit-reveal protocol (extra signed messages + new dispute paths), and a naive seed is grindable.

So every one of those sensations ships — **caused by a player, not by a die**:

| Owner's ask | Deterministic implementation |
|---|---|
| **Crit** | **You won the clash read.** Already in the engine (advantage multiplier). Just *sell* it: big gold number, crit sting, camera punch. A crit means you out-read them. |
| **Miss** | **Someone made you miss.** The `Tripper` stun (miss a go) and `Float Like a Butter Cream` (evade → the next incoming attack whiffs). Also fizzle-on-death: you swung at a corpse. |
| **Double attack** | **A move property, not a proc.** A rare Speedy move simply strikes twice (`hits: 2` in the move table — a compile-time field, zero wire cost). |

If the owner later insists on true randomness, it IS feasible — seed from BOTH sides' blind-committed
orders (neither can grind what they cannot see) — but it costs commit-reveal in the channel phase.
**Not now.**

## Turn order is systematically unfair (owner-spotted) — FIX

*"who goes first, seems same side goes first every time, it should probably alternate so it's more
fair."* **Correct, and it is a real bug.** The resolve order is `(speed DESC, side ASC, slot ASC)` —
so **on a speed tie, side 0 acts first every round of every duel.** In a mirror match side 0 wins
every tie and lands every kill first. For a mode that will **stake treats**, that is indefensible.

**Fix:** the tie-breaking side **alternates by round**. Order becomes
`(speed DESC, side == (round % 2) first, slot ASC)`.
Deterministic, total, and **zero state** (`round` is already in the state buffer). Must be pinned by
a golden vector, because it changes resolve order on ties.

## Block must be worth taking

Owner: *"what's the point doing block, unless blocks all i guess."* **Correct — this is a live bug.**
Block is a feeble −40% that protects only the blocker, which is why nobody picks it and why **four
q4 Blocking moves are currently strictly worse than q1 ones**. Fix: raise the block value, and give
the Blocking corner real jobs — **Guard** (intercept a hit aimed at an ally), **Shield** (absorb a
flat chunk), **Counter** (reflect). A dead corner also means the pentagon is really a 4-way, so
every clash read is noisier than it should be.

## Wave 3 — status effects (the only ones that cost state)

**Stun / "miss a go"** (owner's ask), **poison (DoT)**, **slow/haste**, **attack-down**.
Reserve the bytes in Wave 1's version bump; implement after Waves 1–2 prove the sustain axis is fun.
Canonicality rule: **one `u8` turn-counter per status** (0 = absent). A shared flags+duration byte is
NOT canonical (two encodings for one logical state) and the buffers get hashed/signed.

## Wave 1b — INFORMATION (the cheapest depth in the whole design)

Owner: *"when choose the attacks, we see a lot more info for the attacks, what it does, what it's
best against. also the treats weaknesses etc."* — and, on card-ifying the moves: *"nah is fine how
is"* → **keep the current move-button HUD; enrich it.**

This is **not just UI**. The research finding: *in a blind-pick game the information comes from the
OPTION SET, not the choice.* Public loadouts + public cooldowns give you poker's exact structure —
you know their **range**, not their **hand**. That converts the clash pentagon from a coin flip into
**inference**, which is the difference between luck and skill. The data already exists; we simply
aren't showing it. Ship this in Wave 1, before any new verb.

**A. Rich move info (on the picking HUD, for the move you're about to choose):**
- **What it does** — the per-move `description` (owner's ask), authored in `duel-stats.json`.
- **What it's best against / loses to** — the clash pentagon, spelled out in words, not just a
  colour. (The owner has now asked twice what the colours mean → the game must say it.)
- Power · speed (who acts first) · cooldown · effect (AoE / heal / siphon / stun / guard …).

**B. Derived treat "weakness" (there is no intrinsic element in this game — it is derived):**
A treat has **no** type of its own; its vulnerability is **read off its loadout**. If an enemy's
moves are mostly Heavy, then Tricky and Distance beat Heavy → *"vulnerable to Tricky / Distance"*.
Show, per enemy treat:
- its **move pool** (what it *could* do — its range),
- which of those are **on cooldown right now** (what it *cannot* do this round — this is the
  load-bearing one; it narrows the range and is already in state),
- the **derived weakness/strength** summary computed from the still-available moves.

**C. Own-team readout:** the same, so you can see what your treat is currently good/bad into.

Design constraint: **legible to a casual player.** Plain words ("Strong vs Speedy & Blocking"), not
a matrix. Progressive disclosure — a tap/hover expands the detail; the button stays clean.

## Move → ability mapping (names drive it)

Owner: *"can check the names for what we can change them to."* The existing names are a gift —
**no renaming needed**; we add a **description** per move (owner's suggestion) in
`duel/data/duel-stats.json` (zero on-chain change).

**q1 (12) — plain honest damage, no effects.** The reliable baseline a newbie can win with.
Exceptions by name: `Sugar Shield` → small self-shield; `Gum Drop Kick` → plain block stance.

**q2 (7) — one simple rider:**
`Chewy Absorption` → **life siphon** · `Chilling Blow` → **slow** · `Cinnamon Blast` → **small
splash** · `Float Like a Butter Cream` → self-shield/evade · `Come Over Here and Slip on it` → slow ·
`Choco Crunch`, plain heavy damage.

**q3 (7) — real utility:**
`Blackout Shot` → **stun** · `Toffee Tripper` → **stun (trip)** · `Berry Bounce` → **counter** ·
`Candied Shell` → **shield** · `Limon Shuriken` → **multi-hit** · `Sugar Rush` → **haste / self-heal** ·
`Vicious Jawbreaker` → big single-target burst.

**q4 (13) — signature/exotic:**
`Golden Shower of Chips` → **AoE (sprays everyone)** · `Conductive Coat` → **AoE (chains)** ·
`Gold Rush` → **AoE volley** · `Explosive Jawbreaker` → **AoE splash** · `Gilded Tripper` → **stun** ·
`Barfing Blow` → **poison (DoT)** · `Gilded Bonds` → **Guard an ally** · `Bouncing Barrage` →
**counter** · `Tarnising Knee Drop` → **attack-down (tarnish)** · `Heavy Gumdrop Kick` → **team
shield** · `Super Sugary Rush` → **team haste / group heal** · `Quicksilver Slice` → **always acts
first** · `Silver Knuckles` → huge single-target execute.

## Staging the action (3D) — owner's ask

*"if attacking i wonder if the treat should walk over to the one it's attacking and do the move
there… if healing or whatever then turn to face whomever is getting healed."* **Yes — this is what
sells the combat.** Frontend-only (`src/render/DuelStage.tsx`), zero engine impact:

- **Attacker walks to its target**, plays its move animation *there*, then returns to its slot.
- **Healer/guardian turns to face the ally** it is helping.
- **Distance moves stay put** — the projectile already flies (that's their whole identity).
- **AoE** strides to the centre of the enemy line and sweeps.
All of it is driven off the existing `TimelineStep` seam; the resolve order already gives us a
one-actor-at-a-time stage, so nobody overlaps.

## Fairness model (for staking / capture)

- **Bracket duels by sweetness**, exactly like tournaments already do (a known, understood concept).
- **Damage stays flat** across quality + sweetness (never scale it — that is a *power* lever, not a
  *depth* lever, and it is the pay-to-win vector).
- **Drop / shrink the quality-HP bonus** (currently up to +90) so that within a bracket everyone is
  roughly equally durable, and an Epic's *entire* advantage is its toolkit.
- Net: a fair fight you can safely put a treat on the line for.

## Landmines found (must fix regardless)

1. **`kLogCap = 8192` silently truncates.** Sized for 10 single-target actions. AoE at 5v5 = ~50 log
   entries ≈ 7,050 B. `jsonout.h` drops past-cap writes **silently by design**, yielding truncated
   JSON → hard throw at `JSON.parse(readLog())` in the client. **Raise to 32 KiB AND make truncation
   return rc −1.**
2. **Round-cap tiebreak is by total HP.** The instant heals/shields exist, turtling to the cap is a
   win button. Retune (e.g. total damage dealt) **in the same change as the first heal**.
3. **KO permanence rests on the `hp == 0` guards.** An unguarded heal silently becomes a revive.
4. **Speed buffs must take effect NEXT round** — the resolve order is frozen before any action runs;
   re-sorting mid-loop is a nondeterminism hazard.
5. **AI (`ai.ts`) must learn the new verbs**, or it will be trivially exploitable.

## Testing

Golden vectors + native↔wasm byte-parity are the gate (`build.sh test|golden|parity|sanitize`).
Every new verb needs: a golden vector, a canonical-encoding rejection test, and an
overflow/bounds test (integer-only, u16 HP, /256 math). FE mirrors (`wire.ts`, `duel-stats.ts`) and
the wasm sha must be republished in lockstep (`publish-fe.sh`).
